# **2. Overview of Project Pi**

## **2.1. Experimental Motivation**

The central question of Project Pi was:

> *Does an AI model require semantic content to reconstruct meaning, or is structural patterning sufficient?*

To test this, researchers designed experiments that systematically removed semantic information from texts while preserving grammatical and structural relationships.

---

## **2.2. Experimental Design**

The experiments involved three main transformations:

- **Semantic erasure**  
  All meaningful words were replaced with random tokens (e.g., “glorp”, “tavik”, “murn”).

- **Structural preservation**  
  Syntax, grammar, punctuation, and relational patterns were preserved.

- **Contextual reconstruction**  
  Models were asked to infer the original meaning or generate coherent interpretations.

Example transformation:

```
Original: The scientist analyzed the data to confirm the hypothesis.
Erased:   The glorp taviked the murn to flaven the dristikon.
```

Despite total semantic erasure, models reconstructed:

- the agent (“scientist”),  
- the action (“analyzed”),  
- the object (“data”),  
- the purpose (“confirm the hypothesis”).
