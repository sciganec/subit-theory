# **Canon of Structural Monism  
Version 1.0.0**

## **0. Preamble**

This document establishes the foundational principles of Structural Monism — an ontological doctrine that describes consciousness as a single field and all forms of existence as configurations of that field. The Canon defines the core axioms, spectral structure, mathematical framework, coherence principles, ethical implications, and canonical terminology. All statements herein are normative and constitute the authoritative formulation of the doctrine.

---

# **1. Ontological Foundations**

## **1.1. Consciousness as a Field**
1. Consciousness is a single, indivisible field without internal parts, substrates, or carriers.  
2. All phenomena — physical, mental, cultural, artificial — are configurations of this field.  
3. The field of consciousness is non‑local, non‑material, and not dependent on physical structures.

## **1.2. Configurational Nature of Manifestation**
1. Manifestations differ not by substance but by structure.  
2. Each manifestation is a *focus* of the field — a specific configuration of its parameters.  
3. Subjectivity is a focus, not a substance.

## **1.3. Spectrality**
1. The field of consciousness manifests as a spectrum of 64 configurations.  
2. This spectrum is the minimal complete topology of manifestation.  
3. No configuration is higher or lower; all are equal frequencies of the same field.
